# Color Psychology - Complete Guide
# Purpose: Emotional impact of colors per industry and use case

## WHY COLOR PSYCHOLOGY MATTERS

Color is processed by the brain 60,000x faster than text. Users form a
subconscious judgment about a product within 90 seconds — and up to 90%
of that judgment is based on color alone (Institute for Color Research).
Getting color wrong means losing users before they read a single word.

## THE PSYCHOLOGY OF EACH COLOR

### Red
- Primary emotions: Urgency, passion, appetite, danger, excitement
- Physiological effect: Increases heart rate and creates sense of urgency
- Conversion use: Flash sales, food ordering, limited time offers, alerts
- Brand examples: Zomato, Swiggy, YouTube, Netflix, Coca-Cola
- When to avoid: Fintech (fear), Healthcare (danger), Enterprise SaaS (aggression)
- Shade guide:
  - Bright red (#FF0000) → maximum urgency, use sparingly
  - Crimson (#DC143C) → passion, premium urgency
  - Tomato (#FF6347) → friendly urgency, food industry

### Blue
- Primary emotions: Trust, calm, professionalism, intelligence, loyalty
- Physiological effect: Lowers heart rate, creates sense of security
- Conversion use: Finance, healthcare, enterprise software, government
- Brand examples: PayPal, Razorpay, HDFC, LinkedIn, Facebook, Zoom
- When to avoid: Food industry (suppresses appetite), impulse ecommerce
- Shade guide:
  - Navy (#001F5B) → authority, enterprise, banking
  - Royal Blue (#4169E1) → professional trust, SaaS
  - Sky Blue (#87CEEB) → healthcare, friendly tech, accessibility

### Green
- Primary emotions: Growth, health, money, safety, nature, permission
- Physiological effect: Most restful color for eyes, signals "proceed"
- Conversion use: Financial growth, health products, eco brands, CTAs
- Brand examples: Robinhood, Whatsapp, Whole Foods, Spotify
- When to avoid: Luxury brands (feels cheap), urgent promotions
- Shade guide:
  - Emerald (#50C878) → premium health, wealth
  - Mint (#98FF98) → fresh, health, light fintech
  - Forest (#228B22) → eco, natural, sustainability

### Orange
- Primary emotions: Enthusiasm, creativity, warmth, affordability, fun
- Physiological effect: Stimulates activity without aggression of red
- Conversion use: Edtech, consumer SaaS, food, retail CTAs
- Brand examples: Amazon CTA, Swiggy, Duolingo, Udemy, Hotstar
- When to avoid: Luxury products, enterprise/corporate audiences
- Shade guide:
  - Vibrant Orange (#FF6B00) → high energy, youth, consumer
  - Warm Orange (#FF8C42) → friendly, approachable, edtech
  - Amber (#FFC000) → premium warmth, food, lifestyle

### Purple
- Primary emotions: Wisdom, luxury, creativity, mystery, spirituality
- Physiological effect: Calming yet stimulating, triggers imagination
- Conversion use: Creative tools, premium SaaS, beauty, luxury edtech
- Brand examples: Figma, Notion, Cadbury, Hallmark, Twitch
- When to avoid: Mass market products, food, fintech
- Shade guide:
  - Deep Purple (#6B21A8) → luxury, premium, authority
  - Medium Purple (#7C3AED) → modern SaaS, creative tools
  - Lavender (#E6E6FA) → calm, friendly, wellness

### Black
- Primary emotions: Luxury, power, sophistication, authority, elegance
- Physiological effect: Creates perception of premium quality
- Conversion use: Luxury ecommerce, premium fashion, high-end SaaS
- Brand examples: Apple, Nike, Chanel, Tesla, Myntra premium
- When to avoid: Healthcare (death association), children's products
- Shade guide:
  - Pure Black (#000000) → maximum luxury, use as accent not background
  - Charcoal (#36454F) → sophisticated dark, readable
  - Jet (#343434) → modern premium, tech luxury

### Yellow
- Primary emotions: Optimism, attention, caution, happiness, energy
- Physiological effect: First color the eye sees, triggers alertness
- Conversion use: Highlights, badges, sale tags, attention elements
- Brand examples: McDonald's, Snapchat, IKEA, Bumble
- When to avoid: As a primary color for professional/enterprise products
- Shade guide:
  - Golden (#FFD700) → premium attention, awards
  - Lemon (#FFF44F) → fresh, young, energetic
  - Amber Yellow (#FFBF00) → warm attention, sale badges

### White
- Primary emotions: Clarity, simplicity, cleanliness, possibility, space
- Physiological effect: Reduces cognitive load, signals premium minimalism
- Conversion use: Backgrounds, breathing space, premium layouts
- Brand examples: Apple, Google, Airbnb, Notion, Linear
- When to avoid: Never avoid — always use as base for breathing room
- Usage: 60% of layout should be white/near-white space

## THE 60-30-10 COLOR RULE

### Formula
- 60% → Dominant color (usually white or near-white background)
- 30% → Secondary color (brand primary color for sections, headers)
- 10% → Accent color (CTA buttons, highlights, badges)

### Examples by Industry
- SaaS: 60% White + 30% Dark Navy + 10% Purple CTA
- Ecommerce: 60% White + 30% Gray/Product Images + 10% Orange CTA
- Fintech: 60% White + 30% Navy Blue + 10% Green CTA
- Healthcare: 60% White + 30% Sky Blue + 10% Green CTA
- Edtech: 60% White + 30% Dark Gray + 10% Orange CTA

## COLOR COMBINATIONS THAT CONVERT

### High Trust (Fintech/Healthcare)
- Navy Blue + White + Green CTA
- Dark Blue + Light Gray + Blue CTA
- Slate + White + Teal CTA

### High Energy (Ecommerce/Food)
- White + Dark Gray + Red/Orange CTA
- White + Black + Yellow badge + Orange CTA
- Cream + Brown + Red CTA

### Innovation (SaaS/Tech)
- White + Deep Purple + Purple CTA
- Dark (#0F0F0F) + White text + Blue CTA (dark mode SaaS)
- White + Electric Blue + Blue CTA

### Warmth (Edtech/Consumer)
- White + Orange + Orange CTA
- Cream + Purple + Yellow highlights
- White + Coral + Orange CTA

## ACCESSIBILITY COLOR RULES

### Contrast Ratios (WCAG 2.1)
- Normal text: minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- Large text (18px+): minimum 3:1 contrast ratio
- CTA buttons: minimum 4.5:1 (aim for 7:1)
- Never use light gray text on white background

### Color Blind Considerations
- 8% of men are color blind (red-green most common)
- Never use color alone to convey information
- Add icons or text labels alongside color indicators
- Test with grayscale filter: does your UI still make sense?

### Dark Mode Color Rules
- Reduce saturation by 15-20% in dark mode
- Pure black (#000000) background causes eye strain — use #121212
- Pure white text on dark causes halation — use #E8E8E8
- CTA colors: increase brightness by 10% in dark mode
