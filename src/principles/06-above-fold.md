# Above The Fold Psychology - Complete Guide
# Purpose: Hook users in 3 seconds before they scroll or leave

## THE 3-SECOND RULE

Microsoft research shows average attention span online is 8 seconds.
Nielsen Norman Group data shows users decide to stay or leave within
3 seconds of landing on a page. Above the fold is the only guaranteed
real estate — everything below requires a user to choose to scroll.

You have 3 seconds and one screen. Use them perfectly.

## THE 5 MANDATORY ABOVE-FOLD ELEMENTS

### Element 1: The Headline
- Job: Answer "What is this?" in under 8 words
- Must be the largest text on the page
- Font size: minimum 40px desktop, 28px mobile
- Must be immediately understandable — no jargon, no cleverness
- Test: Show to a stranger for 5 seconds. Can they repeat what you do?

### Element 2: The Sub-Headline
- Job: Answer "Who is this for and what result do they get?"
- Expands on headline with audience + outcome
- Font size: 18-22px desktop, 16px mobile
- Maximum 2 lines
- Formula: "For [audience] who want to [goal] without [pain]"

### Element 3: The Primary CTA
- Job: Give user exactly one action to take
- Must be visible without scrolling on all device sizes
- High contrast color against background
- One CTA only — two equal CTAs cause paralysis
- Below CTA: risk reduction text (no credit card, free forever, etc.)

### Element 4: The Trust Signal
- Job: Reduce skepticism before user has read anything
- Options ranked by impact:
  1. Social proof number: "Trusted by 50,000+ businesses"
  2. Logo strip: 5 recognizable brand logos
  3. Star rating: "4.9 ★ from 2,000+ reviews"
  4. Press mention: "Featured in TechCrunch, YourStory"
  5. Award badge: "#1 Product of the Day on Product Hunt"
- One trust signal above fold is enough — do not clutter

### Element 5: The Hero Visual
- Job: Show the product or the result, not abstract metaphors
- Options ranked by conversion impact:
  1. Product screenshot/mockup (SaaS, apps) → shows exactly what they get
  2. Result photo (before/after, outcome) → triggers desire
  3. Person using the product → creates identification
  4. Video thumbnail with play button → highest engagement
  5. Abstract illustration → lowest impact, avoid

## ABOVE FOLD LAYOUT PATTERNS

### Split Layout (Most Common for SaaS)
- Left 50%: Headline + Sub-headline + CTA + Trust signal
- Right 50%: Product screenshot or demo video
- Works because: Text on left matches F-pattern, visual on right adds context

### Centered Layout (Works for Consumer/Brand)
- Center: Logo + Headline + Sub-headline + CTA
- Background: Full-width image or video (with overlay for readability)
- Works because: Simple, direct, no distraction

### Full-Screen Video Background
- Background: Autoplay muted video showing product in use
- Overlay: Dark semi-transparent layer (opacity 0.5-0.6)
- Center: Headline + CTA
- Warning: Slows page load — use only if video is critical to understanding

### Announcement Bar Above Fold
- Thin bar above navigation: "🎉 New: [Feature] — [CTA link]"
- Adds urgency or news without disrupting main fold
- Color: Brand accent or contrasting color
- Dismissable: Always include X button

## HEADLINE FORMULAS THAT WORK

### Formula 1: The Direct Benefit
"[Do X] [faster/cheaper/better/easier]"
- "Send invoices in 30 seconds"
- "Learn coding in 6 weeks"
- "Pay vendors instantly"

### Formula 2: The Problem Solver
"Stop [pain]. Start [gain]."
- "Stop chasing payments. Start getting paid on time."
- "Stop guessing. Start knowing what your users want."

### Formula 3: The Social Proof Headline
"[Number] [audience] use [product] to [result]"
- "50,000 freelancers use Razorpay to get paid faster"
- "1 million students learned to code with us"

### Formula 4: The Bold Claim
"The [superlative] way to [desired outcome]"
- "The fastest way to build a landing page"
- "The only HR tool built for Indian startups"

### Formula 5: The Question
"[Struggling with pain]?"
- "Still chasing clients for payments?"
- "Tired of losing sales at checkout?"
- Question headlines work because they trigger self-identification

## ABOVE FOLD MISTAKES — RANKED BY DAMAGE

### Critical Mistakes (Fix Immediately)
1. No clear headline → user cannot understand what you do → immediate exit
2. CTA below fold → user never finds the action → zero conversion
3. Hero image showing abstract art → does not show product → confusion
4. Headline in brand language user does not understand → immediate exit
5. Page takes more than 3 seconds to load → user leaves before seeing fold

### Serious Mistakes (Fix This Week)
6. Two equal-weight CTAs → decision paralysis → neither gets clicked
7. Navigation too large → dominates fold → pushes content down
8. No trust signal above fold → skepticism goes unchallenged
9. Background image makes text unreadable → message lost
10. Font too small → user has to lean in to read → bad first impression

### Minor Mistakes (Fix This Month)
11. Generic stock photo of people shaking hands → no differentiation
12. Sub-headline just repeats headline in different words → wasted space
13. No mobile above-fold audit → mobile users see different fold
14. Social proof number not updated → "50 customers" on a 5-year-old product

## ABOVE FOLD BY INDUSTRY

### Ecommerce Product Page
- Hero: Product image (multiple angles, zoom capable)
- Headline: Product name + key benefit
- Trust: Rating + review count + "Free returns"
- CTA: "Add to Cart" + "Buy Now"
- Scarcity: "Only 4 left" badge if genuine

### SaaS Landing Page
- Hero: Product screenshot or animated demo GIF
- Headline: Core outcome in 6-8 words
- Sub-headline: Who it's for + how it works
- Trust: Logo strip or user count
- CTA: "Start Free Trial" + "No credit card required"

### Fintech Homepage
- Hero: Dashboard screenshot or mobile app screen
- Headline: Trust-focused outcome
- Sub-headline: Security + simplicity message
- Trust: RBI license badge + transaction volume
- CTA: "Open Account" or "Get Started"

### Healthcare Platform
- Hero: Doctor photo or patient + doctor interaction
- Headline: Health outcome focused
- Sub-headline: Convenience + credibility
- Trust: Doctor count + consultation count + rating
- CTA: "Book Appointment" or "Talk to a Doctor"

### Edtech Platform
- Hero: Student success photo or course preview
- Headline: Career outcome focused
- Sub-headline: Timeframe + credibility
- Trust: Student count + placement rate
- CTA: "Start Learning Free" or "Enroll Now"
- FOMO: Cohort start date + seats remaining

## ABOVE FOLD TESTING FRAMEWORK

### What to Test First
1. Headline copy → biggest impact on clarity and conversion
2. CTA copy → "Start Free Trial" vs "Get Started Free" vs "Try For Free"
3. Hero visual → product screenshot vs person using product vs video
4. Trust signal type → number vs logos vs rating
5. CTA color → brand color vs high-contrast accent

### How to Measure
- Primary metric: Scroll depth (do users scroll past fold?)
- Secondary metric: CTA click-through rate
- Tertiary metric: Time on page
- Tool: Hotjar heatmaps + Google Optimize A/B tests
