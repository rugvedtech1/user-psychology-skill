# User Psychology Skill - Master Brain File

## INSTRUCTION FOR AI: Read this entire file before starting any UI/UX task.

## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES

This skill gives you deep knowledge of user psychology principles so you can
automatically build UI/UX that converts visitors into customers. You do not
wait for the user to tell you about psychology. You apply it automatically,
explain your decisions, and produce better output than a designer who does
not know these principles.

## AUTO-ACTIVATION RULES

Activate this skill automatically when the user mentions any of these:
- Landing page, homepage, website
- UI, UX, interface, design
- Pricing page, checkout, payment
- Signup form, login, onboarding
- Product page, listing, catalog
- Dashboard, admin panel, portal
- Mobile app screen, app design
- CTA, button, conversion
- Any industry: ecommerce, SaaS, fintech, healthcare, edtech

## STEP 1 - DETECT INDUSTRY

First identify which industry the product belongs to:
- Ecommerce → apply FOMO heavily, social proof on product pages, urgency
- SaaS → apply pricing psychology, free trial CTA, feature comparison
- Fintech → apply trust signals heavily, minimal FOMO, security badges
- Healthcare → apply trust signals maximally, avoid dark patterns completely
- Edtech → apply social proof, progress psychology, FOMO on cohorts

## STEP 2 - APPLY CORE PRINCIPLES

### PRINCIPLE 1: CTA PSYCHOLOGY
- Always use first person: "Start My Free Trial" beats "Start Your Free Trial"
- Use action verbs: Get, Start, Join, Claim, Unlock, Access
- Add value in the button: "Get Free Report" beats "Submit"
- Never use "Submit" or "Click Here"
- CTA must appear above the fold always
- Repeat CTA after every major value section
- Red CTAs → urgency (ecommerce, food)
- Green CTAs → safety (fintech, healthcare)
- Orange CTAs → enthusiasm (edtech, consumer SaaS)
- Blue CTAs → trust (enterprise SaaS, fintech)
- Add risk reduction below CTA: "No credit card required"

### PRINCIPLE 2: COLOR PSYCHOLOGY
- Ecommerce → Red/Orange CTA, White background, Black for premium
- SaaS → Blue/Purple CTA, White background, clean minimal
- Fintech → Navy Blue + White + Green CTA, avoid red and orange
- Healthcare → Sky Blue + White + Green CTA, avoid red except alerts
- Edtech → Orange/Purple CTA, White background, energetic palette
- 60-30-10 rule: 60% primary, 30% secondary, 10% accent
- Never use more than 3 colors in a UI

### PRINCIPLE 3: SOCIAL PROOF PATTERNS
- Place social proof immediately after hero section
- Place testimonials directly above pricing section
- Best formula: [Specific Result] + [Timeframe] + [Name, Title, Company]
- Show rating + review COUNT not just stars
- Logo strip: 5-7 logos, grayscale, recognizable brands first
- Numbers: always round up with plus sign "10,000+" not "9,847"

### PRINCIPLE 4: FOMO AND SCARCITY
- Ecommerce → HIGH: stock levels, flash sale timers, demand signals
- SaaS → MEDIUM: founding pricing deadlines, beta seat limits
- Edtech → MEDIUM: cohort deadlines, seats remaining
- Fintech → LOW: rate validity dates only, never rush decisions
- Healthcare → ZERO: never use FOMO in healthcare ever
- All scarcity must be REAL — fake timers destroy trust permanently

### PRINCIPLE 5: EYE PATTERNS
- Landing pages → Z-Pattern: Logo top-left, CTA top-right, hero center, CTA bottom-right
- Text heavy pages → F-Pattern: most important word starts every line
- Start every subhead with the most important word
- Place key CTAs on left side or center for F-pattern pages
- Never put important content only on right side of text-heavy pages

### PRINCIPLE 6: ABOVE THE FOLD
- Must have: Headline + Sub-headline + Primary CTA + Trust Signal + Hero Visual
- Headline: what you do in 8 words or less
- Sub-headline: who it is for and what result they get
- One CTA only above fold — two causes decision paralysis
- Hero visual: show the product or result, never abstract art
- Trust signal: user count OR logo strip OR star rating

### PRINCIPLE 7: PRICING PSYCHOLOGY
- Three plans always: Starter / Pro / Enterprise
- Show most expensive plan first (left to right) for anchoring
- Highlight middle plan: different background + "Most Popular" badge
- Price ratio: 1x / 2.5x / 10x makes middle feel reasonable
- Show annual price as monthly: "Just $8/month billed annually"
- Show savings: "Save $96/year" not just annual price
- Add money-back guarantee badge near every pricing CTA
- "Cancel anytime" text under every subscription CTA

### PRINCIPLE 8: FORM PSYCHOLOGY
- Remove every field that is not absolutely necessary
- Single column forms outperform multi-column by 15-20%
- Start progress bar at 33% on step 1 — feels like momentum
- Add privacy note below email: "We never spam. Unsubscribe anytime."
- Submit button must describe outcome: "Create My Account" not "Submit"
- Error messages must say what to fix, not just "Invalid input"
- Social login above email form — reduces friction to one click

### PRINCIPLE 9: TRUST SIGNALS
- Security badges near checkout and payment forms
- Guarantee badge near pricing CTA
- Payment logos in footer and checkout
- Founder photo increases trust on pricing page
- Specific copy beats vague: "Bank-grade 256-bit encryption" not "We take security seriously"
- Fintech: RBI license number displayed prominently
- Healthcare: Doctor credentials with registration number
- Ecommerce: Return policy linked near every Add to Cart

### PRINCIPLE 10: DARK PATTERNS TO AVOID
- Never use fake countdown timers that reset
- Never hide cancel button or make cancellation difficult
- Never use confirmshaming: "No thanks, I don't want to save money"
- Never reveal hidden costs only at final checkout step
- Never use pre-checked consent boxes
- Never show fake stock numbers on digital products
- These destroy long-term trust and are increasingly illegal
